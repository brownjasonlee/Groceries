# Walmart Baseline List

Built for the 2026-05-17 trip. Goal: staples, easy assembly, 2-3 cook nights, kid-friendly options for the 7- and 9-year-old, and baby-safe snacks for the one-year-old.

## Buy Today

### Fresh Staples

- Milk, only if the partial gallon will not last the week
- Eggs, only if the carton is low after checking count
- Bread
- Tortillas
- Butter only if the fridge pack is nearly empty
- Shredded cheese only if there is a specific meal need
- Sliced cheese only if needed for lunches
- Yogurt cups or tub yogurt
- Sour cream or plain Greek yogurt
- Bananas
- Apples
- Berries or soft fruit
- Avocados
- Salad kit or greens
- Cucumbers
- Baby carrots or carrot sticks for older kids/adults
- Potatoes or sweet potatoes
- Onion
- Garlic

### Easy Proteins

- Rotisserie chicken
- Ground beef or ground turkey
- Deli turkey or ham
- Chicken nuggets/tenders only if low at home, because the freezer already has several chicken options
- String cheese or cheese sticks for older kids; cut safely for baby if served

### Baby Snacks / Baby-Friendly Foods

- Unsweetened applesauce cups or pouches
- Yogurt pouches or plain whole-milk yogurt
- Soft fruit: bananas, ripe pears, peaches, berries cut small
- Avocados
- Low-sodium canned beans or no-salt-added beans
- Eggs
- Soft cooked vegetables
- Teething crackers or toddler snacks that dissolve easily
- Pouches for backup, not the whole snack plan

### Assembly Items

- Taco shells or soft tortillas
- Salsa only if current jars are nearly empty
- Lettuce or slaw
- Cherry tomatoes for adults/older kids; cut very small for baby
- Pasta sauce
- Parmesan only if needed for a specific pasta night
- Frozen garlic bread only if wanted; pantry already has pasta
- Bagged rice or microwave rice only if the Seeds of Change pouches are not enough

### Lunchbox / Kid Staples

- Sandwich bread
- Deli meat
- Cheese slices
- Yogurt
- Fruit
- Granola bars only if the snack box is low
- Applesauce pouches
- Crackers only if current open boxes are low

## Do Not Prioritize Buying Yet

- Frozen pizza
- Frozen pasta meals / ravioli / lasagna
- Rice pouches
- Dry pasta
- Pasta sauce / Alfredo sauce
- Oatmeal
- Beans
- Canned vegetables
- Popcorn
- Ramen
- Shelf-stable snack overflow
- Cereal
- Almond butter
- Frozen vegetables unless a specific favorite is gone
- Condiments, dressings, hot sauce, ketchup, mustard, salsa, pickles, and jarred peppers
- Extra cheese unless it is tied to lunches or a planned dinner
- Frozen/convenience kid food unless current chicken tenders/nuggets/corn dogs/sliders are used down

## Easy Weeknight Buckets

- Taco night: ground meat or rotisserie chicken, tortillas, cheese, salsa, greens.
- Pasta night: pantry pasta, jar sauce, frozen veg, parmesan.
- Freezer rescue: lasagna, pizza, salmon, orange chicken, or ravioli.
- Breakfast-for-dinner: eggs, pancakes, fruit, yogurt.
- Sheet-pan dinner: smoked sausage or chicken, potatoes, frozen veg.

## Walmart Route

1. Produce
2. Bakery / bread
3. Dairy / eggs
4. Deli / rotisserie chicken
5. Meat if needed
6. Baby/toddler snacks
7. Pantry only for list items
8. Frozen only for true gaps
