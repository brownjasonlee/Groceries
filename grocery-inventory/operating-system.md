# Grocery Operating System

## Household Assumptions

- Groceries and meal planning are now owned here.
- Laundry is separate and happens Sunday at the laundromat.
- Most weeks need 2-3 actual cook nights.
- Only about 2 of those cook nights usually include the 7- and 9-year-old.
- There is a baby a little over one year old who is starting to eat regular food.

## Weekly Rhythm

### Sunday Reset

- Take photos of fridge, freezer, pantry, and overflow storage.
- Check the use-up list before making the Walmart list.
- Buy fresh staples and only fill true gaps.
- Put receipt photos in `receipts/`.
- Put cart photos in `cart-photos/`.

### Midweek Check

- Check milk, eggs, bread, fruit, yogurt, diapers/wipes if relevant, and baby snacks.
- Use freezer meals for schedule pressure instead of ordering out by default.

## Inventory Rules

- Do not track every single snack packet long-term.
- Track categories and restock thresholds instead.
- Pantry/freezer overflow means the next list should favor fresh items and use-up meals.
- If an item is bought three weeks in a row, promote it to staple.
- If an item sits untouched for a month, stop buying it unless it has a specific planned use.

## Reorder Thresholds

- Milk: buy when less than half full.
- Eggs: buy when less than 6 remain.
- Bread/tortillas: buy when one usable package remains.
- Fresh fruit: buy weekly.
- Yogurt: buy weekly if kids/baby are eating it.
- Easy protein: keep at least one rotisserie/chicken/ground-meat option available.
- Freezer meals: do not restock until current pizza/lasagna/ravioli/salmon options are used down.

## Baby Food Safety Notes

For the baby, default to soft foods cut small and served while seated. The CDC lists common choking risks for young children, including uncut grapes/berries/cherry tomatoes, hard raw fruit or vegetables, spoonfuls of nut butter, hot dogs/sausages, chips/pretzels/popcorn, and similar foods. CDC guidance also says to cut cylindrical foods like hot dogs/sausage/string cheese into short thin strips and cut small round foods like grapes, cherries, berries, and tomatoes into small pieces.

Source: CDC Infant and Toddler Nutrition, choking hazards and solid foods guidance:

- https://www.cdc.gov/infant-toddler-nutrition/foods-and-drinks/choking-hazards.html
- https://www.cdc.gov/infant-toddler-nutrition/foods-and-drinks/when-what-and-how-to-introduce-solid-foods.html

## Receipt / Cart Intake

When new photos arrive:

1. Save them under the dated receipt or cart folder.
2. Pull out item names, quantities, and prices when visible.
3. Add new staples to the weekly baseline if they are likely repeats.
4. Add one-off or treat items only to the dated trip notes.
5. Reconcile purchased items against the current inventory.
